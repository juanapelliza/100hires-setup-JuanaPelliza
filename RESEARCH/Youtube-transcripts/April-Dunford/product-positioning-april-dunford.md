## Summary
- **Positioning is Context:** April explains that positioning is the act of setting the context for your product so that its unique value becomes "obvious" to your target customers.
- **The 5 Components:** She breaks down her framework: Competitive alternatives, Unique attributes, Value, Target customer segments, and Market category.
- **Selling the "New Way":** Effective positioning isn't just about describing a product; it’s about framing a new way of solving a problem that makes the old way look obsolete.

## Key Insights
- **The "Better" Trap:** Don't try to be "better" than the market leader. Be "different" in a way that solves a specific pain point for a specific group. If you're just "better," the leader always wins on brand recognition.
- **Value vs. Features:** Features are what your product has; Value is what the customer *gets*. Good positioning builds a bridge between the two using context.
- **Your Real Competitor is the Status Quo:** Most SaaS companies don't lose to direct competitors; they lose to "doing nothing" or a messy spreadsheet. Your positioning must highlight the cost of staying the same.
